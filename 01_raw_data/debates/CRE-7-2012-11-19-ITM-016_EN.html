Verbatim report of proceedings
Monday, 19 November 2012 - Strasbourg OJ edition

16. Order of business
Video of the speeches
PV
 

  President. − The final draft agenda as drawn up pursuant to Rule 137 of the Rules of Procedure by the Conference of Presidents at its meeting of 15 November 2012 has been distributed. The following changes have been proposed:

Monday:

 
  
 

  Sophia in 't Veld (ALDE). – Mr President, I am not sure if this is related to the agenda, but I do not know where else to raise it. This House has the habit of expressing its solidarity and paying respect to victims of human rights violations. A couple of weeks ago in an EU Member State, a woman died unnecessarily of pregnancy-related complications. She was denied – on religious grounds – an abortion that could have saved her.

I think that this is a violation of human rights. It is the right to life that was denied to that woman, and I think that it would be appropriate for this Parliament to pay its respects to the family of the victim.

(Applause)

 
  
 

  President. − That is certainly not relevant to the agenda, Ms in ‘t Veld. I am deeply disturbed by the death of Mrs Savita Halappanavar in such questionable circumstances, but I am sure you will appreciate that, in my capacity as President of this House, I cannot comment on matters that are sub judice. However, I think the reaction of the House has shown how Members feel about this case, and you may be sure that the European Parliament will monitor further developments with the keenest attention.

Tuesday:

The Group of the European People’s Party (Christian Democrats) has asked that we conclude the debate on the oral question concerning the EU steel industry, scheduled for Tuesday afternoon, with the tabling of motions for resolutions.

 
  
 

  Amalia Sartori (PPE). – (IT) Mr President, ladies and gentlemen, those of us in the Committee on Industry, Research and Energy have submitted a question for oral answer to this Parliament, to be dealt with tomorrow afternoon. The motion from the Committee on Industry, Research and Energy was approved unanimously, by 44 members out of the 44 who were present, and on this occasion it became clear that it would be beneficial to seek a resolution.

I know that the Bureau has decided in the first instance not to accept our motion. Mr President, I would like to ask this Chamber and you if we could review this position, since we believe that Europe is currently going through extremely difficult times in many sectors, but particularly in steel and steelmaking, given that jobs in this sector fell by 10.6 % during 2011 and, indeed, there is talk of another reduction by 10.4 % this year.

For this reason, I think it would be beneficial to everyone to send a signal to the Commission and to Europe if, after tomorrow’s debate, we could already think of passing a resolution in December.

 
  
 

  Enrique Guerrero Salom (S&D). – (ES) Mr President, I am not surprised that the Committee on Industry, Research and Energy unanimously approved an oral question and debate on this matter, because the issue of European industrialisation, or indeed re-industrialisation in certain areas which have abandoned industry, is crucial for the future of the Union, for its progress and for innovation.

I believe that this matter that we are going to debate should prompt us to talk about the steel problem, particularly in certain regions in Europe, but also about the industrial programme for the future of the European Union. My group would be in favour of a resolution provided that it is actually approved at the next plenary sitting in December.

 
  
 

  Reinhard Bütikofer (Verts/ALE). – (DE) Mr President, ladies and gentlemen, I find it quite right that we agreed in the Committee on Industry, Research and Energy to enter this topic on the agenda. However, I do not see what is to be gained if we now hold over our discussion of the matter for a month, when both previous speakers have described it as urgent. If you feel that drafting a resolution will help to resolve the problems, then I am not sure I can agree, to put it diplomatically.

I think we should make this an urgent public issue. That is what we would suggest, and we shall undoubtedly return to the matter in future. However, postponing our resolution on the matter hardly implies that it is urgent; instead, it looks more like Parliament indulging in a spot of navel-gazing.

 
  
 

  President. − If I understood her correctly, Ms Sartori does not want the debate to be held over to December but merely the vote on the resolution. Therefore we will have our debate now but hold the vote during the December plenary.

(Parliament agreed to the request)

 
  
 

  Daniel Cohn-Bendit (Verts/ALE). – (DE) Mr President, ladies and gentlemen, I need very few words to make clear to you the precariousness of the situation in Gaza. Very few words to explain that we stand on the brink of war here – not just between Israel and Palestine, but war involving the whole region. Therefore I think it is important that Parliament should now state its position, should say clearly where it stands so that we can make it clear to the Council – the Council will also be addressing the matter – and to forthcoming summits what Parliament thinks about this. We cannot postpone it. This quarrel is dangerous, and I think it is our duty, as a politically engaged body, to adopt a clear stance on it through the medium of a resolution.

 
  
 

  Gabriele Zimmer (GUE/NGL). – (DE) Mr President, my group also thinks that Wednesday’s debate on the statement which the Council will be making on the situation in the Middle East should be accompanied by a resolution. We believe it would act as a guide for Lady Ashton on what we as the European Parliament expect, what the European Union – holder of the Nobel Peace Prize – can do to calm the situation in the Middle East and help to save lives.

 
  
 

  Enrique Guerrero Salom (S&D). – (ES) Mr President, my group would first like to join with you in expressing its deep regret at the recent loss of human life in Gaza which is still continuing at this time; it believes, along with the representatives of the Group of the Greens/European Free Alliance and the Confederal Group of the European United Left – Nordic Green Left, that this is a matter on which Parliament has to adopt a position, and it should do this during the debate scheduled for next Wednesday, by telling the High Representative verbally what the position of this House is.

We would also support the tabling of a formal resolution setting out the position of each group as expressed in a vote. However, in view of the speed at which events in the area are taking place, we would like to have time to consider this resolution properly and would prefer it to be voted on at the plenary sitting in December.

To sum up, we would like to debate this issue thoroughly next Wednesday with the High Representative and to put off until December the voting on the resolution.

 
  
 

  Francesco Enrico Speroni (EFD). – (IT) Mr President, ladies and gentlemen, I believe it is right to vote on the resolution – after all, the content will depend on a variety of factors – but I am against having the vote in December.

Therefore I am in favour, but I think we should vote at this part-session, given the urgency.

 
  
 

  Guy Verhofstadt (ALDE). – (FR) Mr President, I think it is impossible to have a debate without a conclusion. It could, for the moment, be a short resolution condemning violence. It is the least that this Parliament could do. All the other considerations can be the subject of a second resolution later, in December.

I shall therefore support the option for a very short resolution condemning the violence which is currently the cause of so many deaths in the region.

(Applause)

 
  
 

  Enrique Guerrero Salom (S&D). – (ES) Mr President, I would be in favour of it being a very short resolution, which could be negotiated swiftly and would send out a clear message, irrespective of any broader debate and more complex resolutions at a later date.

 
  
 

  President. − I think this is clear: Mr Cohn-Bendit and Mr Zimmer both want the debate and the vote on the resolution to be held during this plenary.

(Parliament agreed to the request)

(The order of business was thus established)(1)

 
  

(1) For other changes to the order of business: see Minutes.
