Verbatim report of proceedings
Tuesday, 17 January 2012 - Strasbourg OJ edition

7. Election of the Vice-Presidents of the European Parliament (continuation)
Video of the speeches
PV
 

  President. – The results of the ballot in the election to the office of Vice-President of the European Parliament are as follows:

Votes cast: 703

Invalid voting slips: 14

Valid votes cast: 689

The absolute majority is thus 345 votes.

I shall announce the number of votes cast in the alphabetical order of the candidates’ names.

Alexander ALVARO: 199 votes

Roberta ANGELILLI: 316 votes

Isabelle DURANT: 182 votes

Othmar KARAS: 305 votes

Miguel Angel MARTÍNEZ MARTÍNEZ: 276 votes

Edward McMILLAN-SCOTT: 225 votes

Georgios PAPASTAMKOS: 324 votes

Gianni PITTELLA: 306 votes

Anni PODIMATA: 269 votes

Jacek PROTASIEWICZ: 267 votes

László SURJÁN: 256 votes

Indrek TARAND: 104 votes

Alejo VIDAL-QUADRAS: 325 votes

Oldřich VLASÁK: 196 votes

Rainer WIELAND: 268 votes

I find that no candidate has achieved the necessary absolute majority of the votes cast in the first ballot. Because all 14 positions are still not filled, a second ballot is required. I would like to ask the candidates whether they all wish to remain as candidates in a second ballot. Is there anyone who wishes to withdraw his or her candidacy?

No one has come forward. We shall then present the same list of candidates in the second ballot. All the candidates in the first ballot remain. We shall now begin with the process of a second ballot.

The results will be published this evening, but if there is a third vote, it will take place tomorrow.

The result of the ballot will be announced before the end of the day. The third ballot will take place tomorrow.

(The Members of the House rose to vote)

 
  
 

  Sergio Paolo Francesco Silvestris (PPE). – (IT) Mr President, ladies and gentlemen, this is not really a point of order, but I am amazed that a vote at the Parliamentary Assembly should be held in conditions that do not objectively guarantee a secret ballot.

It is unthinkable to have to vote in a House surrounded by Members who are able to see how their neighbours are voting. Every Parliamentary Assembly has booths installed at voting time in order to allow a secret ballot. I am not asking for this to be done if the ballot takes place tomorrow, but I would be pleased if you would bear my comments in mind for the future.

 
  
 

  President. – Thank you, Mr Silvestris, I take note of what you have said.

That concludes the ballot. The result of the ballot will be announced this evening. If a third ballot is required, it will be held tomorrow morning.

(The sitting was suspended at 21.40 and resumed at 22.40)

 
  
  

IN THE CHAIR: MARTIN SCHULZ
President
