Verbatim report of proceedings
Wednesday, 6 July 2005 - Strasbourg OJ edition

22. Global call to action against poverty: Making Poverty History
  

- Before the vote on Amendment 2:

 
  
 		 

  Miguel Angel Martínez Martínez (PSE). – (ES) Mr President, I believe that this is self-evident. I would like our friends in the Group of the Greens to accept this amendment, which simply takes note of a statement and does not endorse it fully until we have precise and detailed knowledge of the report that they are referring to.

 
  
  

(The President noted that there was no opposition to the oral amendment)

- on recital A:

 
  
 		 

  Miguel Angel Martínez Martínez (PSE). – (ES) Mr President, I would like to deal with a concern of another group: rather than talking about 300 million, or 315, or 400 million people suffering hunger in the world, we can say ‘more than 300 million’. By doing this, I believe we will avoid giving a precise figure, as other Members wanted.

 
  
  

(The President noted that there was no opposition to the oral amendment)

 
  
 		 

  Bernd Posselt (PPE-DE). – (DE) Mr President, there is a serious matter that I want to bring before you. Mr Rack was right: the House used to have a strict rule about voting times. This is not about whether or not one manages to eat, but I do have visitors here, who have travelled a thousand kilometres for a meeting with me, and after that, I have to be back in the plenary for three o’clock.

We made these rules, and I really do ask that we keep to them in future. We cannot vote on everything on a Wednesday just because there are some lazy people who think they have to leave on that day. We could just as well have voted on the last five reports on Thursday at lunchtime.

I ask the Bureau to plan and organise things properly in the way they used to.

 
  
  

IN THE CHAIR: MRS KAUFMANN
Vice-President

 
  
 		 

  President. Thank you, Mr Posselt; your intervention has been noted and the points you have made will be passed on to the services.

That brings the voting to a close.
