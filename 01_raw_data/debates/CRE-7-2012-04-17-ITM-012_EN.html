Verbatim report of proceedings
Tuesday, 17 April 2012 - Strasbourg OJ edition

12. Order of business
Video of the speeches
PV
 

  President. – The final draft agenda for this part-session, as drawn up by the Conference of Presidents at its meeting on Monday, 12 March 2012, in accordance with Rule 137 of the Rules of Procedure, has been distributed.

The Group of the European People’s Party (Christian Democrats) has tabled a motion requesting that the Council and Commission statements on the legal security of European investments outside the European Union should be put on the agenda. Following these statements, there will be a round of speakers from the political groups.

 
  
 

  Joseph Daul, on behalf of the PPE Group. – (FR) Mr President, you are aware of what is happening in Argentina as concerns the threat of nationalising YPF, a company in which the Spanish group Repsol is a shareholder. We are calling for a debate with a resolution because we wish to protect European investments in third countries and, more specifically, to react to the unilateral and arbitrary decision taken by the Argentinean authorities. We are calling for this debate to take place tomorrow and for a resolution to be drafted that we can work on and later put to the vote. We do not need to adopt it tomorrow. We can do it on Thursday. That is our group’s request.

 
  
 

  Jaime Mayor Oreja (PPE). – (ES) Mr President, there is one principle that we should defend both inside and outside the European Union, and that is the principle of legal certainty. Arbitrariness is an aspect of this that we should be able to face up to and fight against, both inside and outside the European Union.

When a decision is dominated by legal uncertainty and arbitrariness, that constitutes an unlawful action which affects thousands of small shareholders and which, most of all, sets a bad precedent for many European investments around the world. We therefore maintain that this debate is necessary and essential, not only for Spain and for a Spanish company, but also as a future reference for the many European investments made outside our borders.

 
  
 

  President. – Mr Swoboda, I understood from what you have said that you are also in favour of the motion.

(Parliament approved the motion)

The Council and Commission statements on the legal security of European investments outside the European Union will be put on the agenda on Wednesday afternoon.

The deadlines are as follows: for motions for resolutions – Wednesday at 12.00, for amendments and joint motions for resolutions – Thursday at 12.00, for amendments to joint motions for resolutions – Thursday at 13.00.

The vote will take place on Friday, 20 April 2012.

 
  
 

  José Ignacio Salafranca Sánchez-Neyra, on behalf of the PPE Group. – (ES) Mr President, I think we should congratulate ourselves on having made the decision to hold a debate and adopt a resolution. However, bearing in mind that Baroness Ashton will not be present in Parliament tomorrow due to a commitment she has with NATO defence ministers, I believe it would be appropriate to alter the agenda so that we could hear her speak, because she intends to make a statement on that very issue.

 
  
 

  President. – I am very surprised, of course, Mr Salafranca. I was informed that your group wanted its own proper agenda item. We have just established this in detail and have already decided on it. This is why I am surprised by your additional motion. However, Baroness Ashton is naturally free to go into this subject in the statements that she will be making here shortly.

The Group of the Progressive Alliance of Socialists and Democrats in the European Parliament has tabled a motion requesting that the report by Ms Lulling on the taxation of energy products and electricity should be deferred.

 
  
 

  Hannes Swoboda, on behalf of the S&D Group. – (DE) Mr President, the report by Ms Lulling is a very important and very good report. However, in the current situation, it runs the risk of being misunderstood. It is about the greening of the tax system and, in particular, the energy tax system and the taxes on petrol and diesel as vehicle fuels. In this context, there will definitely be concerns in some countries that this report will lead to vehicle fuels, and particularly diesel, becoming more expensive, against the background of fuel prices that are already very high. It is true that the report makes it clear that this is not intended to happen today, but at a later date. The report also states, although somewhat vaguely, that there will be compensation for increases in diesel prices.

I believe that, in the interest of Ms Lulling and of our common environmental awareness and policy making, we should defer this report, so that we can look again at how we can clarify the situation with regard to diesel in particular. This is why I am tabling this motion. I understand that Ms Lulling is sceptical, but it would be in her interest and in that of her report if we could make the wording clearer and then submit it to Parliament at a later date.

 
  
 

  Alexander Graf Lambsdorff, on behalf of the ALDE Group. – (DE) Mr President, I support the motion tabled by Mr Swoboda to remove this report from the agenda. Some points in the report are so incomplete that it needs further debate. The current wording of the report makes it clear that diesel would become considerably more expensive. This is definitely not the sort of economic policy signal that car drivers and industry want to see. Therefore, I believe it is right to discuss this report further and to focus on the uncontroversial areas when it is next referred to Parliament.

 
  
 

  Astrid Lulling, rapporteur. – (FR) Mr President, I must tell you that I see no objective reason to agree to the request made by the Group of the Progressive Alliance of Socialists and Democrats in the European Parliament. As you know Mr President, on Sunday, Mr Sarkozy held a meeting at the Place de la Concorde and he urged the crowd on with the words ‘Do not be afraid!’. Like Mr Sarkozy, I am not sure that I will win but I am not afraid either. I must tell you that Parliament’s consultation on this draft directive dates from 29 April 2011. We have discussed it in committee for seven months. My report was adopted on 29 February and it is now the end of April. Extending the deadlines will not achieve anything because the issues at stake are clear.

(DE) Mr Swoboda says that the situation is unclear, but this is not the case. In the committee, the Liberals and the Socialists clearly voted in favour of increasing the price of diesel. I lost the vote in the committee. An amendment has now been tabled to allow us to vote against applying the principle of proportionality.

(The President interrupted the speaker)

I just wanted to explain this in German so that my fellow Members understand that their arguments do not hold water. It is clear that we do not need this kind of signal in the current economic situation. However, this is the signal that they want to send and they have done so in committee.

(FR) Mr President, there comes a time when we stop talking, and we make a decision. That is why I believe that we must make a decision this week.

(Applause)

 
  
 

  President. – Ms Lulling, we would have understood you even in Luxemburgish.

(Parliament rejected the motion)

The motion for a resolution tabled by the Confederal Group of the European United Left – Nordic Green Left in accordance with Rule 90(6) of the Rules of Procedure concerning the agreement on Passenger Name Records (PNR) with the United States will be included in voting time before the vote on the report by Ms in ’t Veld.

Concerning the report by Mr Kaczmarek on the impact of devolution of the Commission’s management of external assistance from its headquarters to its delegations on aid delivery, the deadline for amendments from the rapporteur or 76 Members and for alternative motions for resolutions is Wednesday, 18 April, at 12.00.

(The order of business was thus established)
