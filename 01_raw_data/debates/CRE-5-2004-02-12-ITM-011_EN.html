Verbatim report of proceedings
Thursday, 12 February 2004 - Strasbourg OJ edition

11. Vote
  

Motions for resolutions on cases of breaches of human rights, democracy and the rule of law (Rule 50)

 
  
  

Joint motion for a resolution(1) on Iran

– Before the vote on paragraph 5

 
  
 		 

  Gahler (PPE-DE). – (DE) Mr President, I believe it is paragraph 5 of the resolution which states that observation of the elections did not take place because of the refusal of the Iranian authorities. That requires correction in so far as the request was also made by Parliament. It should therefore be worded in slightly more neutral terms, in that ‘the authorities’ should be replaced by ‘the Iranian side’. May I request that correction. It is merely a matter of factual clarification.

 
  
  

(Parliament agreed to the inclusion of the oral amendment)

(Parliament adopted the resolution)

 
  
  

Joint motion for a resolution(2) on Cambodia

– Before the vote

 
  
 		 

  Swoboda (PSE). – (DE) Mr President, I only wish to move amendments to recitals D and F. In D and again in paragraph 7, it should be political figures rather than opposition members, since three of the five assassination victims belonged to a governing party. And then the date in recital F should be 7 January instead of 4 February. These are technical rather than substantive amendments.

 
  
 		 

  Watson (ELDR). – Mr President, my Group has no problem with the second of those amendments, but we feel that the first weakens the text and therefore we object to it.

 
  
  

(Parliament agreed to the inclusion of the oral amendments)

(Parliament adopted the resolution)

 
  
  

Joint motion for a resolution(3) on the Greek seamen held in Karachi

– Before the vote

 
  
 		 

  Posselt (PPE-DE). – (DE) Mr President, this is also just a small technical amendment, but I do consider it important, because it is, after all, about people in custody. The text refers to Greek and Filipino sailors, but the title refers only to Greek sailors. I believe we agree that the Filipinos should be released too. For this reason, I move that the title be amended accordingly.

(Applause)

 
  
  

(Parliament agreed to the inclusion of the oral amendment to the title)

(Parliament adopted the resolution)

 
  

(1) Tabled by Mr Suominen and others, on behalf of the PPE-DE Group, Mr Barón Crespo and others, on behalf of the PSE Group, Mr van den Bos, on behalf of the ELDR Group, Mr Lagendijk and others, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Mr Marset Campos and Mr Seppänen, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, and Mr Belder, on behalf of the EDD Group, seeking to replace motions for resolutions B5-0080/2004, B5-0084/2004, B5-0088/2004, B5-0094/2004, B5-0098/2004 and B5-0099/2004 with a new text.
(2) Tabled by Mr Nassauer and others, on behalf of the PPE-DE Group, Mr van den Berg and others, on behalf of the PSE Group, Mr Watson and Mr van den Bos, on behalf of the ELDR Group, Mrs Isler Béguin and Mrs McKenna, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group, Mr Herman Schmid, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group, and Mrs Bonino and others, on behalf of the NI Group, seeking to replace motions for resolutions B5-0079/2004, B5-0082/2004, B5-0085/2004, B5-0095/2004 and B5-0097/2004 with a new text.
