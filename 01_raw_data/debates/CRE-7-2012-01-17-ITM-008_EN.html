Verbatim report of proceedings
Tuesday, 17 January 2012 - Strasbourg OJ edition

8. Election of the Vice-Presidents of the European Parliament (continuation)
Video of the speeches
PV
 

  President. – The results of the second ballot in the election to the office of Vice-President of the European Parliament are as follows:

Number of votes cast: 637

Invalid voting slips: 8

Valid votes cast: 629

The absolute majority is thus 315 votes.

I shall announce the results in the alphabetical order of the candidates’ names.

Alexander ALVARO: 176 votes

Roberta ANGELILLI: 241 votes

Isabelle DURANT: 175 votes

Othmar KARAS: 231 votes

Miguel Angel MARTÍNEZ MARTÍNEZ: 257 votes

Edward McMILLAN-SCOTT: 192 votes

Georgios PAPASTAMKOS: 270 votes

Gianni PITTELLA: 275 votes

Anni PODIMATA: 253 votes

Jacek PROTASIEWICZ: 231 votes

László SURJÁN: 203 votes

Indrek TARAND: 90 votes

Alejo VIDAL-QUADRAS: 262 votes

Oldřich VLASÁK: 184 votes

Rainer WIELAND: 216 votes

I find that no candidate has achieved the necessary absolute majority of the votes cast in the second ballot.

Accordingly, all 14 seats in the election of the Vice-Presidents are unfilled.

 
  
 

  Jim Higgins (PPE). – Mr President, would you mind reading out the figures again slowly please? There are only a few of us here.

 
  
 

  President. – I believe the problem is resolved. However, I am happy to read out the result again. I shall continue with the announcement of the other procedural requirements.

Because all 14 positions are still not filled, a third ballot is required. This third ballot will be held tomorrow morning starting at 09.00.

I would draw your attention to the fact that a relative majority is required in the third ballot, in other words, a quorum with an absolute majority is not required, so that candidates will be elected according to the relative majority of the votes cast.

I shall first ask which of the candidates wish to remain as candidates for a third ballot.

(The President asked the candidates present in alphabetical order)

 
  
 

  Indrek Tarand (Verts/ALE). – Mr President, how much time do I need before tomorrow morning? Or in the morning?

 
  
 

  President. − Mr Tarand, please let us know by 08.00 tomorrow morning if you wish to remain a candidate.

House services tell me that we do not need a new deadline for nominations but that the candidates now proposed shall remain proposed for a third ballot unless they expressly withdraw their candidacy.
