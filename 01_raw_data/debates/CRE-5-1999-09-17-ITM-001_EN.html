Verbatim report of proceedings
Friday, 17 September 1999 - Strasbourg OJ edition

1. Adoption of the Minutes of the previous sitting
 		 

  President. – The Minutes of yesterday’s sitting have been distributed.

Are there any comments?

 
  
 		 

  Sakellariou (PSE). – (DE) Madam Present, in the past our Parliament has looked very critically at the situation in Algeria, but our Parliament was also the first which sought contact and dialogue with the Algerian Parliament. The result of yesterday’s referendum gives us cause to rejoice, along with the people of Algeria, who are on the road to reconciliation and striving to achieve peace in their country.

I would like to ask you, Madam President, to convey the congratulations of this Parliament to the President of Algeria.

 
  
 		 

  President. – Thank you, Mr Sakellariou. Your statement was not included in the Minutes, but I am pleased to take note of it.

 
  
 		 

  Evans, Robert J.E. (PSE). – Madam President, with reference to a point I made in the debate on Tampere on Tuesday, it has been brought to my attention by my colleague, Mr Camre, that remarks that I attributed to him were not what he intended and the translation into English, and I believe French, was the exact opposite of what he intended to say. My criticism of him was perhaps unfair. I would just like to put that straight and I have now done so.

 
  
 		 

  President. – Mr Evans, this correction will be made. Thank you.

(The Minutes were approved)(1)
