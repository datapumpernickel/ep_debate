Verbatim report of proceedings
Thursday, 26 May 2005 - Brussels OJ edition

23. Nutrition and health claims in labelling
  

- Before the vote:

 
  
 		 

  Guido Sacconi (PSE). – (IT) Mr President, as I have to make an objection to the order of the vote regarding Article 3a and Article 4, I ask whether I should do so now or wait for the vote on Amendment 29.

 
  
 		 

  President. The order of the vote has been fixed for some time, but we shall take your point when we get to the relevant part of the vote.

- Before the vote on Amendment 99:

 
  
 		 

  Adriana Poli Bortone (UEN), rapporteur. – (IT) Mr President, although I have not requested to speak, I should like to point out to Members the possible reintroduction of Article 4, which reinstates the nutrient profiles that the Committee on the Environment, Public Health and Food Safety in contrast decided to scrap.

I strongly recommend the approval of this amendment insofar as it serves to restore meaning to the regulation as a whole.

 
  
 		 

  Guido Sacconi (PSE). – (IT) Mr President, I will not comment on the merits of this amendment, but I would just like to point out that to vote first on this new Article 3, dealing with Amendments 99 and 32, is perhaps acceptable from a formal point of view, but not from a substantive point of view, insofar as the new Article 3 is nothing but a new version, a ruse to reintroduce the contents of Article 4.

That is a fact, and so much so that if, for instance, you had the courage – or the misfortune – to read Amendment 99 and compare it with Amendment 88, you would see that they are identical, save for a small detail regarding the dates.

I therefore think it more appropriate to vote on the amendments to Article 4 as such, and only afterwards, and if required, to address those relating to the new Article 3, which seeks to replace Article 4 through the back door.

 
  
 		 

  President. The amendments have been tabled in due form and I shall leave it to the wisdom of the House.
