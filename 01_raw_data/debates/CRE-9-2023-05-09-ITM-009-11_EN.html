9.11. New EU Urban Mobility Framework (A9-0108/2023 - Andrey Novakov) (vote)
PV
 

– Before the vote:

 
  
 

  Andrey Novakov, rapporteur. – Madam President, dear colleagues, I know how do you feel when someone is taking the floor in the middle of the votes. This is why I prepared a very short 45—minute speech.

When I started working on urban mobility, I was thinking that it is all about trains, trams, buses, cars, bikes and, yes, about e—scooters. It turns out to be all about rights. The right of every person with disability in Europe to use urban mobility networks. Every mother with a baby carriage to use a good infrastructure prepared for her. It is all about those kids who are willing to ride a bike on a safe bike lane far from cars, trucks and buses.

So if I have to say, yes, it is all about those who are willing to drive car as well. We should not treat them as criminals. Let us not forget that those who are living in other most rural areas have no alternative. They have to move somehow and the car is their only solution.

If I have to wrap up, there is a reason that the word ‘balance’ has a divine region. It is exactly in the middle between material and spiritual. In our days this means: yes, let’s adopt and encourage people to use new technologies, new modes of transport, but not turn our backs to modes of transport who serve us faithfully for decades.
