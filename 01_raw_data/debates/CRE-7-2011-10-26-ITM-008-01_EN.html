Verbatim report of proceedings
Wednesday, 26 October 2011 - Strasbourg OJ edition

8.1. Draft general budget of the European Union - 2012 financial year (vote)
PV
 

Before the vote:

 
  
 

  Francesca Balzani, rapporteur. – (IT) Mr President, ladies and gentlemen, I simply have a technical amendment that is required in order to start the vote. In lines 05 04 05 02 and 05 08 06, the amendments BUDG1207 and BUDG1206, as recorded in the meeting reports, do not include the amendments to the amounts. These should be incorporated and adopted so as to correct the amounts in the draft budget. In relation to Amendment 1207, that means an additional EUR 2.5 million and EUR 3 million in commitment and payment appropriations, respectively, for line 05 04 05 02. In relation to Amendment 1206, that means an additional EUR 1 million in commitment and payment appropriations for line 05 08 06.

However, the notes that follow particular agency budget lines must be adapted to make them consistent with the definitive amounts decided upon.

 
  
 

(The amendment was adopted)

Before the vote on Amendment 1241:

 
  
 

  Miguel Portas, on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group. – (PT) Mr President, the order submitted here is not the order voted on in the Committee on Budgets. As this contravenes Rule 161 of the Rules of Procedure, which stipulates that the proposal furthest from the adopted text is the one that must be voted on first, Amendment 985 must be voted on first. I move that the amendment to be voted on be the one that reduces Members’ expenditure by 5%.

 
  
 

  José Manuel Fernandes, rapporteur. – (PT) Mr President, I would like to say that the way in which voting has been carried out has not been questioned to date, and there are several examples of this in the votes we have had. I can give you several examples of cases in which what was voted on first was the proposal of the Committee on Budgets. In such cases we voted as a bloc, but I can give other examples: in relation to line 19 10 01 01, there was another amendment, but the one that we voted on first was Amendment 1174, from the Committee on Budgets. To give another example, what we voted on in the first instance on line 19 10 02 came from the Committee on Budgets; what we voted on first in relation to line 19 10 04 came from the Committee on Budgets; and the way in which plenary has voted over the years has primarily focused on the votes of the Committee on Budgets, and only then on the votes of Members or parts of committees. This has been the procedure and the precedent, and it is also what we have done to date as regards the report by my colleague, Ms Balzani.

 
  
 

Before the vote on Amendment 1242:

 
  
 

  Miguel Portas (GUE/NGL). – (PT) Mr President, it is very sad that the chance to vote on reducing the money for Members of this House has been taken away.

 
  
 

  President. – Our thanks are due above all to the Committee on Budgets and to all fellow Members who worked on the budget, including the rapporteurs and shadow rapporteurs. It was an effort made by all of Parliament. As you have heard this morning, 16 parliamentary committees submitted opinions on the budget. So it was our joint effort. I would like to thank everyone very much for this.

After the vote:

 
  
 

  Jacek Dominik, President-in-Office of the Council. – (PL) Mr President, honourable Members, the European Parliament has just adopted amendments to the draft budget – to the Council’s position on the draft budget for 2012. I have made detailed note both of Parliament’s and the Council’s positions on the budget, and in accordance with Article 314(4)(c) of the Treaty of Lisbon, I agree for the President of the European Parliament to convene the Conciliation Committee.

 
  
 

  President. – Thank you, Mr Dominik. As we all know, the European Parliament does have such powers under the Treaty of Lisbon – specific powers when it comes to voting and approving the budget. We did not have such powers under the Treaty of Nice. We will begin the conciliation procedure at the appropriate time.
