Verbatim report of proceedings
Monday, 30 June 2003 - Strasbourg OJ edition

4. Order of business
 		 

  President. The final draft agenda as drawn up pursuant to Rules 110 and 110a of the Rules of Procedure by the Conference of Presidents at its meeting of Thursday, 26 June has been distributed. The following changes have been proposed(1):

Tuesday

The Commission has asked that the Oral Question by the Verts/ALE Group on the addition of water and proteins to chicken meat, currently scheduled as the item before the report by Mr Mulder, be brought forward to follow the joint debate on food safety. Are there any objections?

 
  
 		 

  Roth-Behrendt (PSE). – (DE) Mr President, I do indeed have an objection to make to this. That this House takes lawmaking seriously and treats it as being important is good practice. You will be aware that, tomorrow, as the Conference of Presidents has decided in its wisdom, we will first be having an associated second-reading debate on genetically-modified food and traceability, followed by a joint debate on food legislation, part of which will be on a second reading, and then there will be other debates, also on second readings. Without wishing to belittle the oral question put by the Group of the Greens/European Free Alliance, I am convinced that the order of debate proposed by the Conference of Presidents was the right one. I sympathise with the Commission, which evidently has organisational problems, and I am sure – indeed, I have absolute confidence – that it can sort these organisational problems out, and that it will be in a position later in the day to give an answer to this question.

 
  
  

(Parliament rejected the request)

Wednesday

President. As regards the motion for a resolution on trafficking in children and child soldiers included in the vote pursuant to Rule 104a, a group of 32 Members has submitted a written objection pursuant to Rule 104(4), in order to be able to table amendments thereto. If Parliament agrees, this motion for a resolution could be included in the vote on Wednesday and the deadline for amendments set for Tuesday at 10 a.m.

(The President established that there were no objections to the proposal)

 
  
 		 

  Ford (PSE). – Mr President, as you will be aware, the relevant Rule states that there will be a debate and vote. I presume there will be a debate as well, as the Rule specifies.

 
  
 		 

  President. – As I understand it, the request was for a vote on the resolution, not a debate. However, if you wish to have a debate also, this could be added at the end of business on Tuesday, because the agenda for Wednesday is already very full.

(The President established that there were no objections to this proposal)

 
  

(1) Approval of the Minutes of the previous sitting – Membership of Parliament – Documents received – Petitions – Transfer of appropriations – Documents received – Referral to committees – Texts of agreements forwarded by the Council – Other changes to the order of business: see Minutes.
