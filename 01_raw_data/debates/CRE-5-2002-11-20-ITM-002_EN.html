Verbatim report of proceedings
Wednesday, 20 November 2002 - Strasbourg OJ edition

2. Welcome
 		 

  President. – Colleagues, yesterday morning here in this Chamber I remarked that the coming year, in terms of the European enlargement process, will be a year of parliaments and parliamentarians. I refer to the work we need to do, and the work that needs to be done in the parliaments of the Member States and candidate countries. While making a success of enlargement is a key priority, we should not forget our parliamentary relations with the rest of the world. On the contrary, we need to continue and develop relations with elected representatives from other states and not least with the US Congress.

Today, therefore, I am very happy to greet four Congressmen who are in our Distinguished Visitors' Gallery: Mr Capuano, Mr Dooley, Mr Kind and Mr Moran, we warmly welcome you to Strasbourg and wish you a successful visit.

(Applause)

I should also like to point out that some of the Members of Parliament from the delegations from the Accession States are in the gallery today for our important vote.
